DebtPath
Visual, Animation & Sound Design Brief
Designer and Animator Reference — Companion to Phase 2 Strategy
1. Design Philosophy
Everything in DebtPath must answer one question: does this make the person feel capable and in motion, or does it make them feel judged and stuck?

Debt is emotionally loaded. Most finance apps feel clinical, punitive, or cold. DebtPath must feel like a game where the user is winning - even on hard days. The visual and sound language must encode this at every level.

The single most important design rule: Every interaction that moves the user closer to their goal - even logging a minimum payment, even skipping one soda - must feel like winning. Never neutral. Never nothing. Always: you just did something good.

1a. Primary Goal: Stick to the Plan (No Extra Money Required)
The primary win condition for most users is simply making their minimum payment on time, every time. This prevents late fees, stops interest from compounding faster, and protects their credit score. Design must celebrate this just as enthusiastically as extra payments.

	•	'Plan payment made' is a full XP event, Dex celebration, and streak maintenance action
	•	Users who cannot afford even $0.50 extra must never feel like second-class users
	•	The app celebrates consistency above all - 90 days of on-time payments is a bigger deal than one large lump payment

1b. Secondary Goal: Even $0.50/Day Extra Makes a Dramatic Difference
At 22% APR on a $6,000 balance with $150/month minimum: adding just $0.50/day ($15/month extra) cuts 6 months and $340 in interest. Adding $1/day saves 10+ months. Adding $3/day is transformational.

The visual and copy design must make this math visceral and real - not an abstract percentage, but:
	•	'That coffee you skipped just moved your debt-free date from March 2027 to October 2026.'
	•	'You just saved $43 in interest this month. Dex is doing the math dance.'
	•	'$0.50 a day for 12 months = $892 less in total interest paid.'

The $0.50/day impact calculator should always be visible in the app with real numbers from the user's actual balance and APR. Not hypothetical - personalized to them. Update it every time they log even the smallest extra payment.

4b. Flame Animation Specifications
	•	Flame body: SVG path with transform-origin at bottom center. Y-scale oscillates between 0.92 and 1.10. X-rotation oscillates between -2.5deg and +2.5deg. Both on separate timing to prevent synchronization.
	•	Glow layer: Ellipse beneath the flame, blurred 6-10px. Opacity oscillates 0.4-0.9 on a separate 1.4x slower cycle than the flame body.
	•	Spark particles (100+ day streaks only): Small 3x3px circles emitting from upper third of flame. Random upward trajectory, 0.6-1.0s fade-out. 2-4 active particles at any time.
	•	At-risk state: Flame opacity drops to 0.3-0.5 on a 0.4s heartbeat pulse. Color shifts toward cooler orange. Dex appears holding/protecting it.
	•	Flame born animation (day 1): Flame grows from 0 to full size over 0.8s with easing. First frame is a single spark, then ignition.

5. The Dex Mascot — Design & Animation Specifications
Dex is the emotional engine of DebtPath. Every design decision about Dex must pass this test: does this expression/animation make the user feel that Dex genuinely cares about their specific situation? Dex must never feel like a generic mascot doing stock animations.

5a. Character Design Direction
	•	Species: Original character - compact rounded fox or abstract woodland creature. Not a direct copy of any existing mascot.
	•	Proportions: Head 45% of total height. Eyes 35% of face width. Short limbs, round body. Must read clearly at 40x40px (notification size).
	•	Color: Warm orange-amber primary (#E8850A). Cream/white belly and inner ear. Bright expressive eyes (amber or green iris with large pupil).
	•	Style: 2D vector, bold outlines, no gradients in final production art. Lottie-compatible rigging for all animations.
	•	Avoid: Anything that resembles Duolingo's Duo the owl. Avoid green, bird-like forms, or leaf motifs.

5c. Dex Speech Bubble Design
	•	Rounded rectangle, 16px corner radius. White fill. 1px border in Dex Orange (#E8600A).
	•	Tail points from bottom-left of bubble toward Dex's mouth area.
	•	Text appears character by character, 40ms per character. Cursor blinking while typing. This is the typing animation that creates emotional engagement.
	•	Maximum 2 lines of text in bubble. Never truncate - if copy is longer, it displays in a separate screen element.
	•	Bubble entrance: scale from 0.8 to 1.0 over 0.2s with slight overshoot (spring easing).

6. Screen-Level Animation Specifications

6a. Payment Logged Screen
This is the highest-frequency interaction in the app. It must feel satisfying every single time - not just the first time. Variable reward principle: the core animation is consistent, but small random variations keep it fresh.

	•	Trigger: User taps 'Log payment' and confirms amount
	•	Frame 0-0.1s: Button state changes to loading spinner (prevents double-tap)
	•	Frame 0.1-0.4s: Button transforms into checkmark with green fill. Scale 0.9 -> 1.05 -> 1.0 (spring bounce)
	•	Frame 0.2-0.6s: XP number floats up from button center. '+50 XP' in blue, bold Nunito, 18px. Opacity 0->1->0 over 0.6s. Moves upward 40px.
	•	Frame 0.3-0.7s: Dex happy animation fires simultaneously
	•	Frame 0.4-0.8s: Streak flame pulses once (1.2x scale, 0.3s return)
	•	Frame 0.5-1.0s: XP bar increments with smooth transition (not instant jump)
	•	Frame 0.6-1.2s: Interest saved counter updates with animated number roll (if extra payment)

Variable reward injection: On approximately 15% of payments (random), a secondary celebration fires: additional confetti, 2x XP message ('Bonus day! 2x XP!'), Dex celebration state instead of just happy state. This unpredictability is the core of variable reward psychology.

6b. Streak Milestone Screens (3, 7, 14, 30, 60, 100 days)
	•	Full-screen takeover: semi-transparent dark overlay (0.8 opacity) animates in over 0.3s
	•	Card rises from bottom: white card, 20px corner radius, slides up 100% of screen height over 0.5s with spring easing
	•	Confetti burst: 24-36 colored rectangles (7x7px) emit from card top. Colors: Dex Orange, Trust Blue, Achievement Gold, Freedom Green. Random rotation and fall trajectory. 1.5s animation.
	•	Milestone number: Large display text (Nunito ExtraBold, 72px) in Dex Orange animates in with scale 0.5->1.1->1.0 over 0.4s
	•	Dex celebrating: Full celebrate animation plays 2x
	•	Sound: Milestone fanfare (see sound design section)
	•	Dismiss: 'CLAIM [XP AMOUNT]' button. Tapping fires one final confetti burst then closes overlay.

6c. Level Up Overlay
	•	Similar structure to milestone screen but with level badge animation as focal point
	•	Old level badge: fades out to 0 opacity over 0.3s
	•	New badge: scales from 0 to 1.15 to 1.0 over 0.5s. Badge glows gold (Achievement Gold color) on entry.
	•	Level name appears character by character below badge: 'INTEREST FIGHTER' with 30ms per character
	•	Background: Achievement Gold (#D4A017) at 10% opacity as full-screen tint

6d. Debt Paid Off Screen (The Grand Finale)
This screen is the most important in the entire app. It must be extraordinary. Users will remember this moment and tell people about it. It justifies the entire product.

	•	Phase 1 (0-0.5s): Screen flashes white once (like a camera flash). Jarring in a good way.
	•	Phase 2 (0.5-1.5s): Confetti explosion - not card-level, full screen. 60-80 particles. Mix of rectangles and circles. Multiple colors including white and gold.
	•	Phase 3 (1.0-2.0s): 'DEBT FREE' text animates in at 96px Nunito ExtraBold. Dex in full legendary celebration.
	•	Phase 4 (2.0-5.0s): Real numbers appear one by one: 'Total interest saved: $X', 'Months ahead of schedule: X', 'Streak maintained: X days'. Each with a soft ping sound.
	•	Phase 5 (5.0s+): 'Share your win' button appears. Social sharing is a major referral acquisition moment.
	•	Sound: Full 3-second celebration sequence (see sound design). This is the one sound that gets real production budget.

This is the feature that makes secondary-goal users understand why even tiny extra amounts matter. The visual design must make the math feel real, personal, and dramatic - not academic.

7b. The Payoff Date Animation
When a user logs any extra payment, the payoff date must visibly move forward. This is the single most emotionally powerful animation in the app for secondary-goal users.

	•	'Before' date: displayed in muted red-gray, struck through with a diagonal line
	•	Strike animation: line draws from left to right over 0.4s simultaneously with the extra payment confirmation
	•	'After' date: appears below in Freedom Green (#1E7A45) at 120% scale, bounces down to 100%
	•	Dex appears with speech bubble: 'Your debt-free date just moved up [X] days. That was $[amount].'
	•	If date moves by 30+ days from a single payment: full celebration animation fires

7c. The Interest Savings Bar
A visual progress bar showing cumulative interest saved vs. what they would have paid. This bar only ever grows - it never goes down. This is a pure positive feedback loop.

	•	Label: 'Interest you have kept in your pocket'
	•	Bar color: Freedom Green gradient (light to full green, left to right)
	•	Milestone markers at $50, $100, $250, $500, $1000 saved
	•	When bar reaches a milestone: Dex jumps, coin sound plays, text updates: 'You just crossed $100 saved in interest. That is a car payment you kept.'

Master principle: Every sound should be something users look forward to. Never punitive. Never harsh. If a user describes the streak-risk sound as 'stressful' in testing, redesign it. The test: would you want to hear this sound every day? If not, rework it.

11. Screen Layout Principles

11a. Dashboard Layout Priority
The dashboard shows: (1) Dex with today's greeting, (2) streak flame + count, (3) XP bar + level, (4) payoff date + impact counter, (5) today's action button. This priority order must not change.

	•	Dex and streak flame must be visible without scrolling on all phones including iPhone SE (4-inch)
	•	The payoff date must update visibly when any extra payment is logged - even while the user is on this screen
	•	'Log payment today' button must be visible without scrolling and must always be the most prominent interactive element

11b. Information Hierarchy for Numbers
	•	Streak count: Nunito ExtraBold, 48px or larger. Dex Orange. Most visible number on screen.
	•	XP and level: Nunito Bold, 24px. Trust Blue. Secondary prominence.
	•	Debt balance: Nunito Regular, 18px. Neutral dark gray. Never red. Not emotionally loaded.
	•	Interest saved: Nunito Bold, 20px. Freedom Green. Prominently featured but not dominant.
	•	Payoff date: Nunito ExtraBold, 28px. Changes color from muted red-gray to Freedom Green when extra payments are logged.

11c. Accessibility Requirements
	•	All text: minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio against background. Test Dex Orange on white (#FFF) - it passes at 4.5:1 at the standard value.
	•	All tap targets: minimum 44x44pt. Dex himself should be tappable and trigger a random encouraging speech bubble.
	•	Sound effects: all sounds have visual equivalents. XP float-up covers for XP earned sound. Animation covers for all milestone sounds.
	•	Reduce motion: when system 'reduce motion' is enabled, replace bounce animations with simple fade transitions. Keep all functional elements visible.
	•	Font scaling: all layouts must accommodate iOS Dynamic Type up to 'Accessibility Large' without breaking the dashboard layout.
