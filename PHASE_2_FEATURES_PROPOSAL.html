DebtPath - Phase 2 Proposal

Turning DebtPath from a tracker into a guided system

Prepared for Eric  |  April 2026

Hey Eric - here's the plan for the next phase, broken down the way I think about it. The app is in a good place after the v1 work. Phase 2 is about turning it from a useful calculator into something users actually finish their debt journey with.

From watching real user behavior across the other debt apps out there, three problems show up over and over:

  • Knowing what to do next - people want clear guidance, not just numbers.
  • Staying consistent - motivation drops sharply after the first few days.
  • Trusting the outcome - they want clarity on savings, payoff time, and progress.

Phase 2 is built around fixing those three things, and AI is the thing that makes it work.

Why AI keeps the build fast and the price low

Phase 2 is built AI-first. A lot of the work that used to take weeks of hand-coded logic - personalized copy, behavior triggers, content generation, ranking - is now handled by the AI layer at runtime. That's how we keep the engineering scope tight and the price competitive.

  • AI Coach - explains strategy, runs what-if scenarios, recovers lost streaks.
  • AI-personalized notifications - written per user from their actual debt data, not from templates.
  • AI-driven insights - weekly summaries and milestone messages tailored to each user.
  • AI-matched financial opportunities - ranks bonuses and offers by each user's debt and APR profile.

All of these share the same AI foundation - we build it once and it powers everything else.

What users complain about in other debt apps

From App Store and Google Play reviews, Reddit threads, and recent comparison articles. These are the gaps Phase 2 is designed to fill:

What users say

Where it shows up

"It feels like a calculator, not a guide."

Common across Snowball, Undebt.it, and Debt Payoff Planner. Users want a coach, not a spreadsheet.

"I lose motivation after a few days."

Drop-off pattern in App Store reviews of every major payoff app. None of them have a real engagement loop.

"I don't trust the results."

Debt Payoff Planner reviewers say it shows debts as paid off when APR isn't applied. YNAB loan accounts also drift from real balances.

"Nothing pushes me to take action."

Generic reminders get ignored. Users want context-aware nudges like "add $25 today, save $312 in interest."

Predatory or hidden subscriptions.

Bright Money users report unauthorized $97 charges. Rocket Money sits at 1.6 stars on Trustpilot largely because of surprise fees.

Apps shutting down and trapping money.

Tally (closed Aug 2024) and Qoins (closed Jun 2023) stranded user funds. Trust is now a real differentiator.

What we're building

1. AI Smart Coach.  A focused assistant - not open-ended chat. It does three things really well: explains the user's strategy in plain English, lets them ask "what if I add $X?" and shows the new payoff date, and nudges them with practical next steps when they're stuck or behind.

2. AI-Personalized Notifications.  No more generic reminders. Every notification is generated from the user's actual plan - things like "add $25 today and save $312 in interest" or "7 days to your next milestone."

3. Motivation System.  An XP and streak leaderboard (no debt balances shown - users compete on consistency, not on what they owe), a referral system with invite codes, and better streak mechanics including streak-freeze tokens and bonus days.

4. AI-Driven Financial Opportunity Layer.  Surface real bonuses, accounts, and balance-transfer cards that fit each user's situation, with step-by-step guidance so they actually claim them. Tracked outcomes feed back into the payoff math.
